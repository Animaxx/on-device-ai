Information Collection and Use

Our app's core functionality is designed to be 100% on-device, meaning your data is processed and stored locally. However, we now offer an optional feature to connect with third-party cloud-based AI model providers. The use of this feature is off by default and requires your explicit consent and action to enable.

Cloud Provider Integration (Optional)

This app allows you to optionally connect to third-party AI model providers (such as OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Groq, OpenRouter, LM Studio, or Ollama) using your own API keys. This feature is off by default and must be actively enabled by you.

When you choose to use a cloud-based model, the following information will be transmitted from your device directly to the selected third-party provider:

  • Your Prompts and Conversation History: The text you submit and the context of the current conversation are sent to the provider to generate a response.
  • Your API Key: This is sent to authenticate your request with the provider.

We do not collect, view, or store this information on our servers. The transmission occurs directly between your device and the third-party provider's servers.

Data Storage and Security

While the app itself does not store personal information, we securely store API keys, user conversations, and settings locally on your device using advanced security APIs designed to safeguard your data.

AI Models

The On-Device AI app uses machine learning models that run entirely on your device. These models may be downloaded to your device during installation or on-demand. The processing of your requests, including voice recognition and AI responses, happens locally without sending your data to external servers.

App Permissions

To provide its functionality, On-Device AI may request permissions such as microphone access (for voice input) and storage access (to save your data locally). You can manage these permissions through your device settings. We only use these permissions to provide the app's core features.

However, once your data reaches the third-party provider, its security and privacy are governed by their policies and infrastructure. We encourage you to use strong device passcodes and keep your operating system updated.
