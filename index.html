Frequently Asked Questions About On Device AI

Get answers to common questions about privacy, features, and compatibility.

What is On Device AI and how does it work?

On Device AI is a privacy-first AI application that runs powerful language models like Llama, Gemma, Qwen, and DeepSeek directly on your iPhone, iPad, Mac, or Vision Pro. All AI processing happens locally on your device - your data never leaves your device and no internet connection is required for core features.

Can On Device AI work completely offline?

Yes! On Device AI is designed for 100% offline operation. Once you download an AI model, you can use voice transcription, document analysis, AI chat, and all core features without any internet connection. Your conversations and data stay completely private on your device.

What AI models are supported by On Device AI?

On Device AI supports leading open-source models including Llama 3, Gemma 3, Qwen 3, DeepSeek, Phi-4, Mistral, and more. You can also import custom GGUF models from Hugging Face. The app uses both llama.cpp and MLX inference engines for optimal performance on Apple Silicon.

How do Collaborative AI Teams work?

Collaborative AI Teams let you create multi-agent workflows where specialized AI agents work together on complex tasks. For example, you can deploy a Market Analyst, Strategic Planner, and Risk Assessor team for comprehensive business planning, or use an Idea Generator, Story Developer, and Editor team for creative writing projects.

What devices are compatible with On Device AI?

On Device AI works on iPhone 14 and newer, iPad mini (7th gen) and newer, iPad Air (5th gen) and newer, iPad Pro (3rd gen) and newer, iPad (11th gen) and newer, all Apple Silicon Macs, and Apple Vision Pro. Devices with 6GB+ RAM provide optimal performance.

Can I connect cloud AI providers to On Device AI?

Yes! While the app is 100% offline by default, you can optionally connect to cloud providers like OpenAI, Anthropic Claude, Google Gemini, Groq, OpenRouter, LM Studio, or Ollama when you need extra processing power. You can switch between local and cloud models within the same conversation.

What are Knowledge Libraries in On Device AI?

Knowledge Libraries are dedicated AI memory spaces for each project. You can create a Library for each project or client, group related PDFs, notes, web captures, and images together. The AI assistant then answers questions sourced directly from your active Library, providing focused and accurate responses.

Does On Device AI support vision models for image analysis?

Yes! On Device AI supports both local vision models (MLX and GGUF-based) and cloud vision APIs. You can analyze photos, screenshots, diagrams, and documents directly on your device with complete privacy, or use powerful cloud vision models for more detailed analysis.
